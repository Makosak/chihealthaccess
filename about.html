About the Data

Health Risk Indicators

Childhood Opportunity Index

The childhood opportunity index is a measure of educational, health/environmental, and social/economic opportunity across metropolitan neighborhoods.

For details, see here.

Health Literacy Index

Health literacy measures ability to obtain, process, and understand basic health information and services.

For more information, see here.

Economic Hardship Index

The economic hardship index draws on six factors to measure urban economic hardship: community unemployment, dependency, education, income level, crowded housing, and poverty.

For details, see here.

Violent Crime Rate

The violent crime rate measures frequency of violent crime including homicide, aggravated sexual assault, armed robbery, etc.

For the Chicago Police Department's annual reports, see here.

Property Crime Rate

The property crime rate measures frequency of violent crime including robbery, arson, and burgalry.

For the Chicago Police Department's annual reports, see here.

Premature Mortality Rate

The premature mortality is the number of deaths to residents under age 75 per 100,000 persons.
